Methodology

Evaluation process

How rolling forecast origins, direct and recursive inference, availability-aware scoring, model selection, confirmation and the frozen final audit fit together.

Two separate ideas

“Walk-forward” describes the evaluation loop; “direct” and “recursive” describe how a model produces the seven forecast days inside each loop.

Outer loop

Walk-forward / rolling-origin validation

Choose a historical cutoff, train using only information available by that cutoff, predict the following seven days, score after their actuals are revealed, then repeat at another cutoff. Each repetition is an independent out-of-fold test.

Inside one fold

Direct vs. recursive forecasting

Direct predicts horizons 1–7 from the same observed pre-origin history. Recursive predicts one day, appends that estimate as synthetic history, and uses it to construct the next day. Either strategy can be evaluated with the same walk-forward origins.

One walk-forward fold, step by step

The same leakage-safe sequence is repeated for every selected origin.

1 · Freeze the originThe origin is the final day considered known. Quantities, availability states and aggregate-demand features after that date are unavailable to training and feature construction.
2 · Build training dataThe model is fitted only on eligible historical targets at or before the origin. Unavailable-product quantities are treated as censored observations rather than genuine zero demand.
3 · Forecast seven daysThe target block is origin + 1 through origin + 7. Future-known calendar, campaign, discount, price and event information may be used; future realized quantities may not.
4 · Align common rowsCompared models are scored on the same product-date population. This prevents one model from appearing better merely because difficult or missing rows were silently excluded.
5 · Score after the factWAPE is primary; MAE, RMSE, signed bias and bias ratio diagnose scale, tail errors and systematic over- or under-forecasting.
6 · Move the originThe complete process is repeated at another historical cutoff. Models are refitted or baselines recomputed from the information that would genuinely have existed at that time.

Forecast-generation strategies

Both contracts were implemented; the current published submission uses the development-selected winner.

Scoring contract

The metric population is fixed before comparing model scores.

Conditional demand · primaryScore only product-days on which the product was available. This estimates underlying customer demand rather than penalizing a model for inventory censorship.
Realized sales · diagnosticScore every observed product-day, including unavailable days. This measures what was sold under actual stock constraints, not the unconstrained demand target.
Common populationFor a comparison, retain rows with an actual value and valid predictions from every included model. Expected, predicted and scored row counts are reported explicitly.
Global aggregationPool absolute errors and actual demand across the selected rows before computing WAPE. Large-volume product-days therefore receive proportionate operational weight.

Selection objective

Development decisions are optimized for a January-like test week without inspecting test actuals.

Leakage and overfitting controls

Controls that make the reported OOF scores comparable to a real forecast made on January 11, 2026.

Origin-relative featuresRolling demand, market state and ordinary lags are calculated only from observations known at the fold origin.
Target-known covariates onlyFuture calendar, campaign, discount, price and event variables are admitted only because they are supplied or knowable for the forecast dates.
No fold early-stopping leakageThe seven-day evaluation block is never used as an early-stopping or hyperparameter-tuning set for the model being scored on it.
Development-only decisionsFeature, loss, strategy and ensemble-weight decisions are made from development OOF. Recent benchmark and final audit have confirmation roles and do not refit those decisions.

Metric glossary

Why several metrics are retained even though WAPE drives selection.

WAPEΣ|actual − prediction| / Σactual. Primary scale-weighted accuracy measure; lower is better.
MAEAverage absolute unit error. Easy to interpret directly in quantities.
RMSESquare-root mean squared error. Penalizes occasional large misses more strongly than MAE.
BiasAverage prediction − actual. Positive means systematic over-forecasting; negative means under-forecasting.
Bias ratioTotal signed error divided by total actual demand. Makes aggregate directional bias comparable across periods.
CoverageShare of expected rows for which a finite prediction exists. Accuracy is not accepted at the cost of missing difficult rows.
