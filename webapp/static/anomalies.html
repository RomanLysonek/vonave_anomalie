Three layers, three different meanings

An anomaly score is not automatically a bad row and it is not automatically a better forecast.

01

Statistical surprise

Compares each product-day with causal same-weekday history, robustly normalizes the residual and calibrates an extreme tail threshold.

Question answered “Was this observation unusually far from its recent seasonal expectation?”
02

Systemic reconstruction risk

Trains neural autoencoders on multivariate 28–84 day windows and scores demand regimes that are difficult to reconstruct.

Question answered “Does the whole market currently look unlike learned historical regimes?”
03

Forecast contribution

Injects anomaly state as bounded features, weights or specialist models and validates marginal value on walk-forward OOF predictions.

Question answered “Does this information improve WAPE after the main forecaster already sees price, campaign and calendar context?”
